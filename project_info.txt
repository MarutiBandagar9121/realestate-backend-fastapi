Commercial Real Estate Platform – Architecture & Data Modeling Brief
1. Objective

We are building a commercial real estate platform that will support both public property discovery and internal brokerage operations.

The primary objective is to design a robust, scalable, and extensible backend architecture and database model that can power a modern proptech platform operating across multiple commercial real estate verticals.

The system must:

support diverse property asset types

support multiple commercial transaction models

scale to large datasets

integrate with external CRM systems

remain maintainable for long-term evolution

We are seeking architectural guidance similar to what would be expected from a senior enterprise software architect designing a large proptech platform.

The most important goal is to design a clean domain model and database structure that balances flexibility, performance, and maintainability.

2. Business Context

Our company operates in the commercial real estate advisory and brokerage industry.

We help businesses discover, evaluate, lease, purchase, and invest in commercial real estate assets.

The platform we are building will serve as the core digital infrastructure supporting:

property discovery

deal management

advisor workflows

documentation management

property analytics

client relationship management integration

The platform will eventually support advanced analytics and AI-based recommendations.

3. Business Verticals

The platform must support multiple commercial real estate verticals including:

Office Space

Traditional enterprise office leasing and purchase.

Shared Office / Coworking

Flexible workspace environments including:

hot desks

dedicated desks

private cabins

managed enterprise spaces

Retail Space

Retail stores in malls, shopping centers, and high street locations.

Industrial Space

Manufacturing and industrial units.

Logistics Space

Warehouses, distribution centers, and logistics parks.

Land Deals

Commercial land parcels for development.

Investment Assets

Commercial real estate offered for investment.

Future investment products may include:

fractional ownership

portfolio investments

REIT-like structures

4. Commercial Offering Complexity

A key domain complexity in commercial real estate is the separation between the physical asset and the commercial transaction model.

The same asset may simultaneously support multiple commercial offerings.

Examples include:

leasing

outright sale

investment opportunities

managed office offerings

For example:

A commercial space may be:

available for lease

available for sale

available for investment participation

The data model must support multiple simultaneous offerings tied to the same asset without duplicating core asset information.

Logistics assets may require:

The architecture must address the challenge of modeling highly diverse attribute sets while maintaining strong data integrity and efficient queries.

7. CRM Integration

Our company currently uses Zoho CRM for managing leads and customer relationships.

The backend platform must integrate with Zoho CRM to synchronize data such as:

leads

client communication

Integration requirements include:

receiving webhook events from Zoho

triggering API calls to Zoho

syncing deal data between systems

associating CRM deals with property data

The architecture should consider data synchronization patterns, eventual consistency, and failure handling.

10. Core Architecture Requirements

The system must prioritize the following architectural goals.

Scalability

The system should support growth to:

thousands of properties

large asset inventories

high query volumes

large CRM datasets

Performance

Property discovery queries must remain performant even with large datasets.

Search queries may involve:

multiple filtering conditions

attribute filtering

location filtering

pricing filters

Data Integrity

The system must enforce:

correct attribute validation

correct relationships between domain entities

consistent offering definitions

Flexibility

The model must allow:

new asset types

new transaction types

new business verticals

new investment products

without major schema redesign.

Maintainability

The domain model should be easy for developers to understand and extend.

Clear separation of concerns is important.
